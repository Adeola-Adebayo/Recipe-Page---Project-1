Nigerian Jollof Rice

A beloved West African one-pot rice dish simmered in a rich tomato-pepper base. It’s smoky, slightly spicy, and perfect for gatherings. This version is beginner-friendly yet delivers party-level flavour.

Recipe Information

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 40–50 minutes
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Tip: Keep the pot covered to trap steam so the rice cooks evenly.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long‑grain parboiled rice, rinsed
  • 4 medium tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 large onion (divided: half blended, half sliced)
  • 2–3 scotch bonnet (adjust to taste)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2–3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tsp each: curry powder, dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika (optional but highly recommended)
  • Salt & black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion until smooth.
  2. Heat oil in a pot; sauté the sliced onion until fragrant. Stir in tomato paste and cook 2–3 minutes.
  3. Pour in the blended mixture. Add curry, thyme, bay leaf, paprika, salt, and pepper. Simmer 10–12 minutes to reduce.
  4. Stir in rinsed rice to coat with sauce, then add stock. Mix, taste, and adjust seasoning.
  5. Cover tightly; cook on low heat 25–35 minutes, stirring once or twice to prevent sticking.
  6. When rice is tender and liquid absorbed, turn off heat and let steam (covered) for 5 minutes. Fluff and serve.

Tips & Notes

For signature party jollof flavour, allow slight browning at the bottom (controlled “smokiness”). If the rice dries out before soft, add a few tablespoons of stock, not water, to keep flavour concentrated.
